Loom — Quick Guide
What Is Loom?
Loom is a quiet, distraction-free writing atelier for composing literary work — stories, essays, poems, and long-form prose — directly inside Akasha. Everything you write is stored as connected semantic chunks, woven into the knowledge graph as you work.
Loom can be used for:
  • short stories and fiction
  • essays and long-form nonfiction
  • poetry and verse
  • worldbuilding and narrative design
  • drafting and manuscript development
  • reflective or literary journaling
Basic Workflow
  1. Log in — your active piece opens automatically
  2. Add a chunk and begin writing
  3. Let the piece auto-save as you type
  4. Explore associations in the right panel
  5. Reorder and revise chunks as the work grows
Login
Enter your name and Akasha passphrase, then press Enter. Your active piece opens immediately.
Your Piece
Loom holds one active piece at a time. When you log in, that piece opens automatically. The title is shown in the header — click it to rename the piece inline.
Writing
Press + Chunk in the header (or New chunk when the page is empty) to create a new chunk. Write directly in the editor. The piece auto-saves as you type. Press Ctrl+S to save immediately.
Each chunk is a self-contained unit you can later move, edit, or connect.
Chunks as Cognitive Units
Loom treats a piece as a sequence of chunks rather than one undifferentiated page. Keep each chunk to a single thought, image, or beat. The left panel lists all chunks in order — click one to select it, drag to reorder.
Axes / Tags
After a chunk is saved, the right panel shows automatically derived axis labels — emotional resonance, themes, time markers, and more — drawn from the knowledge graph. Click any axis chip to open the association drawer, which shows related atoms from the graph. Use the Depth slider to control how far the search reaches.
Associations are live: as the graph grows, the drawer reveals more.
Writing Mode
Press Write in the header to collapse both side panels and leave only the editor — a distraction-free page. Press Write again to restore the panels.

Export & Import
Export… saves the active piece (all chunks, links, and metadata) as an .akasha capsule file. Use this to back up your work or move it to another Akasha instance.
Import… opens a file picker. Select an .akasha capsule file; its atoms are injected into a pending isolation scope for verification. Both File ▾ items are in the header menu.
Suggested Workflow
  1. Draft quickly, one chunk at a time — do not stop to polish
  2. Let the graph tag the work automatically
  3. Follow associations when you are stuck or curious
  4. Reorder and revise chunks once the shape emerges
  5. Re-read after accumulation; let themes and patterns surface
Loom works best when a piece grows over many sittings.
Philosophy
Loom is intentionally lightweight. It is not trying to replace full manuscript suites, publishing platforms, or collaborative editors. Instead, it acts as a semantic writing room for living drafts and evolving ideas.
The goal is not only to store words. The goal is to keep the threads of a work connected — to thought, to memory, to everything else you know — while the writing is still alive.
